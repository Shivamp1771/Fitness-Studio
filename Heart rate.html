Understanding your Target Heart Rate

To identify your personal target heart rate zone, you must first calculate your maximum heart rate. The easiest way to calculate your maximum heart rate is by doing this simple subtraction equation: 220 – your age = your maximum heart rate.

How to Calculate Ideal Heart Rate

Let’s use a 56-year old person as an example:

  • max heart rate = 220 – 56
  • max heart rate = 164
  • Ideal heart rate = max heart rate * .85
  • Ideal heart rate = 164 * .85
  • Ideal heart rate = 139(Approx.)

Understanding your Heart Rate Zone

Your heart rate zone is calculated as a range of 50 to 85 percent of your maximum heart rate. Keeping your pulse within the target heart rate zone during an exercise allows you to receive maximum benefit.
